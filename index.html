GLOBAL AKHANDA GAYATRI

“Never give up the Gayatri; you may give up or ignore any other mantra but you should recite the Gayatri at least a few times a day.”
[Sathya Sai Baba - Upanayanam Day, Brindavan, 20/06/1977],

Dear Brothers and Sisters, Om Sri Sai Ram!


Sathya Sai Baba says that the Gayatri Mantra illuminates our intellect as well as the surrounding atmosphere: “Westerners have investigated the vibrations produced by this mantra and have found that when It is recited with the correct accent as laid down in the Vedas, the atmosphere around becomes visibly illumined. Therefore, Brahma-Prakasha, the Divine Effulgence, will descend on you, illumine your intellect, and light your path when you chant this mantra. In addition, repeat shanti thrice at the end, for that repetition will give shanti or peace to three entities in you: body, mind, and soul.”
[‘Sathya Sai Speaks’, volume 13.34 - June, 20, 1977]

Now, imagine several spiritual seekers all around the world, reciting the Gayatri with love and dedication! They will set in motion a powerful, protecting light wave of blessings, for the benefit of every being on Earth as well as our Mother Earth itself.

Present times are challenging for humanity with all the wars, famine, violence, fear etc. that every nation of the globe experience with varied intensity. As a Divine Countermeasure, we propose chanting of Gayatri Mantra, as a service to our Planet and ourselves.

Akhanda means eternal, uninterrupted. The purpose of this practice is to fill the 24 hours of each day with the sound of Gayatri Mantra, forever, aided by the voices of spiritual seekers in every corner of the globe, spiritual light spots shining throughout the Planet!

May our Beloved Lord Sathya Sai shower His blessings on our efforts and pour His boundless Grace on all beings!

Samasta Loka Sukhino Bhavantu!

Deeper meaning of the Gayatri Mantra

The Gayatri has three parts: 1.Praise, 2.Meditation, and 3.Prayer. First, the Divine is praised, then It is meditated upon in reverence and lastly, an appeal is made to the Divine to dispel the darkness of ignorance and to awaken and strengthen the intellect. Dhīmahi is related to the meditative aspect. Dhīyo Yonah Prachodayāt relates to the aspect of prayer. Chanting of the Gayatri Mantra purifies the mind and confers devotion, detachment and wisdom.

[‘Sathya Sai Speaks’, vol 33.04: Feb, 10, 2000]


Gayatri is the Mother of all scriptures (Vedas). She is present, wherever Her name is chanted. She is very powerful. The One who nourishes the individual being is Gayatri. She bestows pure thoughts on anyone who worships Her. She is the embodiment of all Goddesses. Our very breath is Gayatri, our faith in existence is Gayatri. Gayatri has five faces, they are the five life principles. She has nine descriptions, they are ‘Om, Bhur, Bhuvah, Swah, Tat, Savitur, Vareñyaṃ, Bhargo, Devasya’. Mother Gayatri nourishes and protects every being and She channelizes our senses in the proper direction. ‘Dhīmahi’ means meditation. We pray to Her to inspire us with good intelligence. ‘Dhīyo Yonah Prachodayāt’ - We beseech Her to bestow on us everything we need. Thus Gayatri is a complete prayer for protection, nourishment and finally, liberation.

[‘My Dear Students’, Vol 3, Ch 2, Mar 19, 1998]


It is essential to recite the Gayatri Mantra at least three times during morning, noon, and evening. This will serve to reduce the effects of the wrong acts one does every day. It is like buying goods for cash, instead of getting them on credit. There is no accumulation of karmic (result of action) debt, since each day's karma (action) is atoned for that day itself by reciting the  Gayatri Mantra.

[‘Sathya Sai Speaks’, vol 16.6: March, 17, 1983]
